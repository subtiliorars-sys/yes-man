## Description (short)

Yes Man is a warm, funny idle game about the quiet super-power of saying *yes*.
Tap a friendly YES button, build a chorus of enthusiastic helpers, and spread
Cheer — with no timers, no FOMO, and full offline progress.

## Description (one paragraph)

You tap one big YES and good things compound. A dog who says yes to every
squirrel, a friend who endorses your worst plans, an optimism robot, and
eventually the Cosmic Yes itself keep the Cheer flowing while you live your
life. Between taps, life asks small questions — *help move a couch? try the
weird restaurant?* — and saying yes pays off in gentle, funny ways. It's built
like a gift: generous offline progress, optional prestige, exportable saves,
and zero dark patterns. A game that is genuinely on your side.

## History / Why we made it

Yes Man began as a simple question: what would an idle game look like if it
refused to manipulate you? No artificial urgency, no guilt, no slot-machine
psychology — just craft, comedy, and a quietly profound idea borrowed from
American Pragmatism: *an idea becomes true by what it does.* Saying yes is a
small act with enormous downstream consequences. That is also, conveniently,
the exact shape of a satisfying incremental game.

## Key Features

- One-tap joy with squash, particles, and a soft synth pop.
- 7 enthusiastic auto-yesers, each with a distinct comedic voice.
- 35 "say yes to life" prompts across three unlocking tiers.
- Golden Yes surprise bubbles — delightful, never timed-FOMO.
- 17 stamps + 10 hidden secrets for collectors.
- Optional, never-punishing prestige ("A Fresh Outlook").
- Full offline progress, Reduce Motion + Sound toggles, exportable saves.
